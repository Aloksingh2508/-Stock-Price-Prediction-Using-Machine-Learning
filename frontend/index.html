📚 Terminology & Indicators

📉 RSI (Relative Strength Index)

A 0–100 scale that measures whether a stock has been bought or sold too aggressively. Below 30 = possibly oversold (cheap), above 70 = possibly overbought (expensive).

📊 MACD

Short for "Moving Average Convergence Divergence". It shows whether the stock's short-term trend is above or below its long-term trend. When MACD crosses above the signal line, that's a bullish sign.

〰 Bollinger Bands

Upper and lower price boundaries based on recent volatility. If price touches the lower band, the stock might be undervalued. Touching the upper band could mean it's overvalued.

🧠 LSTM Model

Long Short-Term Memory — a type of neural network that remembers patterns over time. It's the same AI architecture used in voice assistants and language translation tools.

📏 RMSE (Accuracy Score)

Root Mean Squared Error — how far off the AI's predictions were on average (in dollars). A lower number means the model was more accurate on historical data.

🎯 Model Confidence %

How certain the AI is about its recommendation, calculated from how well it fitted the historical data and how strongly the market signals agree with each other.
